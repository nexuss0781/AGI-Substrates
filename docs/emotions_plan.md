 Based on research in evolutionary psychology, affective neuroscience, and developmental psychology, here are the **hardcoded emotions that humans are naturally born with**:

## The Core "Basic Emotions"

Research consistently identifies **6 primary emotions** that appear to be biologically innate, universal across cultures, and present from birth or early infancy :

| Emotion | Function | Evidence of Being Hardcoded |
|---------|----------|----------------------------|
| **Joy/Happiness** | Reward seeking, social bonding | Present in newborns; distinct facial expressions (smiling, cheek raising) observable in infants  |
| **Sadness** | Elicit care/support from others; signal loss | Distinct facial expression (frowning, inner eyebrow raise); present in early infancy  |
| **Fear** | Threat detection, survival | Hardwired subcortical circuits (amygdala, periaqueductal gray); observable in newborns  |
| **Anger** | Defense, boundary setting | Subcortical rage systems; facial expressions present early; observable in infants  |
| **Disgust** | Avoid contamination, toxin avoidance | Crucial for survival; facial expression (nose wrinkle) present at birth  |
| **Surprise** | Orienting response to unexpected events | Distinctive facial expression (widened eyes, open mouth); present early  |

## Panksepp's 7 Primary Emotional Systems

Neuroscientist Jaak Panksepp identified **seven core emotional systems** that are evolutionarily ancient, subcortically located, and shared across mammalian species :

1. **SEEKING** — Exploration, anticipation, enthusiasm
2. **RAGE/ANGER** — Defense, frustration response
3. **FEAR** — Threat avoidance, safety monitoring
4. **LUST** — Sexual attraction, mating
5. **CARE** — Nurturing, parental bonding
6. **PANIC/GRIEF** — Separation distress, social loss
7. **PLAY** — Social learning, motor skill development

These systems are considered "hardwired" because they **survive radical decortication** (removal of higher brain areas) in animal studies, indicating they operate independently of cortical/cognitive processing .

## Evidence These Are Truly Innate

- **Congenital presence**: Newborns display discrete facial expressions of joy, interest, disgust, and distress within days of birth 
- **Cross-cultural universality**: These emotions are recognized across widely different cultures, including isolated tribes 
- **Presence in blind individuals**: People blind from birth display the same emotional expressions, ruling out pure learning/imitation 
- **Neurobiological basis**: Specific subcortical circuits (amygdala, hypothalamus, periaqueductal gray) are dedicated to these responses 
- **Early imitation**: Infants as young as 10 weeks can imitate expressions of fear, sadness, and surprise 

## Important Nuances

While these emotions are hardcoded at a biological level, **their expression and regulation** are heavily shaped by experience, culture, and cognitive development . The modern scientific consensus leans toward an **epigenetic view**: emotions emerge from the interaction of genetic predispositions and environmental input, rather than being purely "innate" or purely "learned" .

Additionally, more **complex emotions** (guilt, shame, pride, jealousy, romantic love) likely develop later through the combination of basic emotional systems with cognitive and social development .
